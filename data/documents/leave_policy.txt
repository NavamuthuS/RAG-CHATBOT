LEAVE POLICY — Acme Technologies Pvt Ltd
==========================================

1. PURPOSE
This policy explains the types of leave available to employees, how leave
is earned, how to apply for it, and the rules around carry-forward and
encashment.

2. TYPES OF LEAVE

2.1 Casual Leave (CL)
Every employee is entitled to 12 days of casual leave per calendar year,
credited at the rate of 1 day per month. Casual leave can be used for
short personal needs and does not require a medical certificate. A
maximum of 3 consecutive casual leave days can be taken at a time without
manager pre-approval.

2.2 Sick Leave (SL)
Employees get 10 days of sick leave per year. For sick leave of more than
2 consecutive days, a medical certificate from a registered doctor must be
submitted to HR within 3 working days of returning to work.

2.3 Earned/Privilege Leave (EL/PL)
Employees accrue 1.5 days of earned leave per month (18 days per year)
after completing 1 year of continuous service. Earned leave must be
applied for at least 7 days in advance, except in emergencies.

2.4 Maternity Leave
Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave as per
the Maternity Benefit Act, applicable for the first two children. This can
be taken up to 8 weeks before the expected date of delivery.

2.5 Paternity Leave
Male employees are entitled to 10 working days of paid paternity leave,
to be availed within 3 months of the child's birth.

2.6 Bereavement Leave
In the unfortunate event of the death of an immediate family member
(spouse, child, parent, or sibling), employees may avail up to 5 days of
paid bereavement leave.

3. CARRY FORWARD AND ENCASHMENT
- Unused Casual Leave lapses at the end of the calendar year and cannot be
  carried forward or encashed.
- Unused Earned Leave can be carried forward up to a maximum of 45 days.
  Earned Leave beyond 45 days will be automatically encashed in the
  December payroll cycle at the employee's basic salary rate.
- Sick Leave cannot be encashed but may be carried forward up to 20 days.

4. HOW TO APPLY
All leave (except emergency sick leave) must be applied through the HR
portal at least 24 hours in advance. Your reporting manager will receive
an approval request. Leave is considered approved only after manager
sign-off in the system, not merely after informing the manager verbally.

5. UNAUTHORIZED ABSENCE
Absence from work for more than 3 consecutive days without approval or
notice will be treated as unauthorized leave and may result in loss of
pay and disciplinary action, including possible termination as per
company conduct rules.

6. PUBLIC HOLIDAYS
The company observes 12 public holidays each year, as per the list
published by HR in January. Employees working on a declared holiday
(with prior manager approval) are eligible for a compensatory day off
within 30 days.

7. LEAVE DURING PROBATION
Employees on probation (first 6 months) are eligible only for Casual
Leave, on a pro-rata basis of 1 day per completed month. Earned Leave and
Maternity/Paternity Leave benefits begin only after confirmation.

8. CONTACT
For any questions about this policy, employees may contact the HR team
through the chatbot, by raising an "Escalate to HR" ticket, or by writing
to hr@acmetech.example.com.
